Correct cleaning of your teeth

Proper tooth brushing and flossing are critical to maintaining good oral hygiene and preventing dental problems such as cavities, gum disease, and bad breath. Here is information on how to perform these procedures correctly:

Toothbrushing:
  • Frequency: You should brush your teeth at least twice a day, preferably in the morning and before going to bed.
  • Duration: Brush your teeth for at least two minutes in each session.
  • Toothbrush: Use a soft to medium bristle toothbrush. Replace the brush every 3-4 months or when the bristles are worn.
  • Toothpaste: Use a fluoride toothpaste to strengthen tooth enamel and prevent cavities.
Brushing Technique:
  • Position the brush at a 45 degree angle towards the gum line.
  • Make smooth, circular movements.
  • Brush all surfaces of the teeth, including the front, back, and chewing surfaces.
  • Do not exert too much pressure to avoid damaging the gums.
  • Tongue: Don't forget to gently brush your tongue to remove bacteria and prevent bad breath.
Cleaning with dental floss:
  • Frequency: You should floss at least once a day, usually before you brush your teeth at night.
  • Type of Dental Floss: You can choose between waxed or non-waxed dental floss according to your preferences. There are also flavored options.
  • Length: Cut approximately 18 inches (45 cm) of dental floss.
Technique:
  • Hold the floss between your middle fingers and thumbs, leaving a small stretch between them.
  • Gently slide the floss between the teeth using a sawing motion.
  • Form a "C" around each tooth and slide the floss up and down to remove plaque and food debris.
  • Make sure you get all the way to the gum line, but be gentle so you don't hurt them.
Other Oral Cleaning Items:

In addition to the brush and dental floss, there are other elements that you can incorporate into your oral cleaning routine:

  • Mouth Rinse: An antimicrobial mouth rinse can help reduce bacteria and freshen your breath. Use after brushing and flossing.
  • Interdental Brushes: These are small brushes designed to clean between teeth and around braces or implants.
  • Tongue Scrapers: Help remove bacteria and debris from the tongue, improving oral health and reducing bad breath.
  • Remember to visit your dentist regularly for exams and professional cleanings. Good oral hygiene is essential to maintaining a healthy smile and preventing long-term dental problems.

Foods and habits to avoid to maintain good dental hygiene

We understand that there are often suboptimal choices to be cautious of, but excessive marketing in today's world often leads to misinformation. Here, we provide insights into unfavorable habits and dietary choices that warrant consideration about your dental health.

Foods to Avoid:
  • Sugary Snacks and Soft Drinks: Foods and drinks high in sugar can cause tooth decay. Bacteria in the mouth feed on sugar and produce acids that can erode tooth enamel.
  • Sticky Candies and Dried Fruits: These can stick to teeth and promote the growth of harmful bacteria. They are often high in sugar.
  • Citrus Fruits: Although fruits like oranges and lemons are healthy, their high acid content can erode tooth enamel. Rinse your mouth with water after consuming citrus fruits.
  • Hard Candies and Ice Cubes: Chewing on hard substances can cause chipped or fractured teeth.
  • Carbonated Drinks: Soft drinks, even diet soft drinks, can be acidic and harmful to teeth. They are also usually high in sugar.
  • Coffee and Tea: These drinks can stain teeth. Adding sugar and acid creams can contribute to tooth decay.
  • Alcohol: Excessive alcohol consumption can cause dry mouth, increasing the risk of tooth decay and gum disease.
Habits to Avoid:
  • Smoking and Tobacco Chewing: These habits can stain teeth, cause bad breath, and increase the risk of oral cancer and gum disease.
  • Nail Biting and Chewing on Objects: These habits can chip or break teeth.
  • Using Teeth as Tools: Using the teeth to open packages or bite nails can damage them.
  • Teeth Grinding and Clenching: Teeth grinding (bruxism) can wear away enamel and cause tooth pain and sensitivity. Consider wearing a mouth guard at night if you grind your teeth.
  • Skip Dental Checkups: Regular dental checkups and cleanings are essential to detect and address dental problems early.
Tips for Better Dental Health:
  • Brush and Floss Regularly: Brush your teeth at least twice a day and floss once a day to remove food debris and plaque.
  • Using Fluoride Toothpaste: Fluoride helps prevent tooth decay by strengthening tooth enamel.
  • Mouth Rinse: An antimicrobial or fluoride mouth rinse can reduce bacteria and strengthen teeth.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking water helps rinse away food debris and bacteria.
  • Chewing Sugarless Gum: Chewing sugarless gum after meals can stimulate saliva production, which helps neutralize acids and clean teeth.
  • Wear a Mouth Guard: If you play sports or grind your teeth at night, consider wearing a mouth guard to protect your teeth.
  • Balanced Diet: Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains. Limit sugary and acidic foods and drinks.
  • Avoid Constant Snacking: Frequent snacking can expose your teeth to sugar and acids throughout the day. Try to stick to regular meal times.
  • Quitting Smoking: If you smoke, quitting can significantly improve your oral health.
  • Regular Dental Checkups: Visit your dentist for checkups and cleanings at least twice a year.

By following these tips and avoiding harmful foods and habits, you can maintain good dental hygiene and promote a healthy smile.
